The _Nology course

The Nology Junior technical analyst (TA1) course was created and design for people from a wide range of backgrounds with no tech experience. So that the students are equipped with the knowledge to become technical analysts, in just 6 weeks. Listed below are the modules covered within the training period.

Build, Test and Deploy in Practice

Goal: learn and experience coding and programming, and taking a product live

Agile and the modern SDLC

Goal: understand the modern project environment

Managing requirements

Goal: Develop a sucessful requirement mindset

Testing through the lifecycle

Goal: understand the value and importance of testing at every stage

The role of data

Goal: Request and analyse data using modern tooling

Professional skills and buisness analysis

Goal: understand the basics of tech workplace

Topics & Language covered so far:

HTML / CSS / Git-Github / Testing / Test Documentation / Database: SQL, no SQL, Excel,Tableau.

Projects

Build and deploy a simple website with multiple pages

Having learnt HTML and CSS coding during my first week at Nology, I was able to put that knowledge towards generating this website. This gave me a greater understanding of the roles developers have and that amount of work that goes into creating working pieces of software.

Complete the Professional Scrum Masters (PSM 1) certification

During my second week, I had learnt about Agile values and The Scrum framework, and how these apply to a workplace environment. I sat the exam and received the Professional Scrum Masters 1 Certification, I was able to get a greater understanding of roles and the work place environment. You can view my certificate below.

Develope a product backlog for a new product and design a sprint

This week’s group task was to a Product Backlog for a new website – HealthWealth, where users can track the symptoms they are experiencing. Given to us was the Product requirements and from this we were to produce a list of Product backlog items on Trello boards. We were also tasked with creating user stories and acceptance criteria for a product, which gave me a better understanding of how to plan out requirements set for product development.

Given a product and its requirements, test it fully and deliver a defect report

The first task of was to deliver individual defect report on a product given its requirements. We then carried out exploratory testing of a website called intergr8HR, to find any bugs/defects with the product and assigned a severity and priotrity to the defects on a trello board. The next task was to create a test plan document, for the same website, creating a plan to ensure that the product would be a high quality, with no defects.

Present a meaningful data story to support a buisness case study

During weeks 4 and 5, I was introduced to Databases, learning about SQL, NoSQL, Excel and Tableau. We were tasked with individually creating a meaniningful data story to support a case study. We were provided with an Excel data set with information on India's State and thier covid cases.
